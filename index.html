VICI.PR
Growing up, Vici.PR got his start in hip hop as a Bboy or a breakdancer. Through breaking and participating in local events he established his own style and affinity for the culture. After events he would always freestyle with his crew outside. Eventually these became more frequent and he decided to take up music seriously. Vici's sound is inspired by the 90s and early 2000s hip hop and R&B he grew up listening to.

Discography

  • Chasing Dayz

    Chasing Dayz is the first collaboration track between Vici.PR and Manchureon. The song is written as a real life representation of both artists hustle to pursue their musical passion while juggling full time jobs.

  • Ain't Worried

    Ain't worried is the first collaboration track between Vici.PR and Sitch. Sitch is based out of the East Coast and Vici is based out of the West Coast. Both artists connected through an online group page and linked up to create a fun summer track. The track was created during the peak of quarantine during the Covid-19 pandemic. The song tells a story of the first time being back outside again.

  • Please Don't Let Me Go

    Please Don't Let Me Go embodies the fun aspect of hip-hop that Vici grew up with. G-funk music is where Vici feels most comfortable in his rap and this song captures his ability.

  • Seen Her Before

    Seen Her Before is the collaboration between Vici.PR and Meel. Both artists grew up together since middle school. They are both heavily inspired by the 90s era of hip-hop. This song draws inspiration from the classic Tupac song "All About You" and showcases the indivudal artists own style.

  • Please Don't Hurt Me

    Vici.PR and Ashya connected when they both participated in the Joy Ruckus Club music video. They recorded this collaboration track in Koreatown before the show.

  • Looking Back

    Looking Back is Vici.PR's first studio album. After 5 years of making music, it is a benchmark in his progression as an artist.
